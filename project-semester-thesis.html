SIMP Topology Optimisation — Method & Implementation
Solid Isotropic Material with Penalisation (SIMP) applied to 4 structural benchmark problems

SIMP Method

  • Density-based method: each element has a pseudo-density ρ ∈ [0,1]
  • Stiffness penalised: E(ρ) = ρp × E₀, where p = 3 (standard)
  • Objective: minimise compliance (maximise stiffness)
  • Constraint: volume fraction ≤ target
  • Filter: sensitivity filter to avoid checkerboard patterns

AM Constraints Implemented

  • Overhang constraint: limit self-supporting angle (e.g. >45°)
  • Minimum member thickness: enforced via filter radius
  • Build direction: vertical (Z-axis) per SLM process
  • Aspect ratio: ratio of element dimensions — key parameter studied

Aspect Ratio Effect on Structural Performance
Parametric study establishing a practical design guideline for AM-constrained topology optimisation

The Core Research Question

When you impose AM manufacturing constraints (overhang limits, minimum member size, build direction) on a topology optimisation, how much structural performance do you sacrifice compared to the unconstrained optimal? And which parameters drive that loss most?

Key Parameter: Aspect Ratio (AR)

  • AR = length / width ratio of the design domain
  • Study varied AR across range [0.5, 5] for each benchmark
  • Three overhang filter types compared per AR: layer-by-layer, front propagation, front propagation improved
AR < 2
Tradeoff ≤ 5% — independent of overhang filter type
AR = 5

●Research
Findings & Practical Guidelines
Translating parametric study results into actionable engineering rules for AM-TO workflows

Primary Findings

  • AM constraints impose a measurable but bounded performance penalty
  • Aspect Ratio is the dominant driver of performance loss — more influential than filter radius or penalty factor at constant AR
  • The tradeoff is consistent across all tested geometries → guideline is generalisable
  • Overhang constraints become critical at AR ≥ 3

Engineering Guideline Produced

  • Maintain AR < 2 for AM-optimised parts — keeps performance loss within 5%
  • If geometry forces AR ≥ 3, budget for 10–25% compliance increase vs theoretical optimum
  • Filter radius has secondary effect — can partially compensate at moderate AR
  • Penalty factor (p) sensitivity: higher p → sharper 0/1 transitions → less sensitive to AR
