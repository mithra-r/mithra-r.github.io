Selected projects

MASTER'S THESIS  ·  TU MUNICH / DLR  ·  2024–2025

Characterizing lamination parameters realizable domain for practical composite layups

Derived analytical expressions for the realizable domain of lamination parameters under four design guidelines — disorientation, damage tolerance, contiguity, and balance. Validated against brute-force enumeration of symmetric laminates. Integrated into a bi-level optimization framework and benchmarked under multiple load cases.

69.3% safety factor mismatch reduction (tension-dominant); complete elimination for coupled shear/compression load cases.
Python IPOPT DLR Lightworks CLT Bi-level opt.

B.ENG. PROJECT  ·  RVCE BANGALORE  ·  2020–2021

Shape optimization of drain holes in a gas turbine shaft

Minimized stress concentration around drain holes in an Inconel 718 gas turbine shaft across five critical engine operating points. Tested elliptical, square, and rectangular cutout geometries at varying orientations (15° intervals) under combined axial, torsional, and rotational loads. Performed mesh convergence study and validated against analytical stress concentration factor.

Rectangular hole with aspect ratio 2–2.5 yields minimum maximum principal stress; optimal orientation governed by axial/torque load ratio.
Abaqus CAE CATIA V5 HyperMesh Inconel 718

B.ENG. PROJECT  ·  RVCE BANGALORE  ·  2020–2021

Influence of lattice parameters on fatigue life of 18% Ni steel

Characterized retained austenite in heat-treated 18Ni Grade M250 steel via X-ray diffraction using Williamson-Hall analysis. Performed high-cycle fatigue testing on dog-bone specimens on a Universal Testing Machine at three aging temperatures (510°C, 540°C, 620°C). Correlated lattice parameters and austenite percentage with fatigue life.

Austenite % increases with aging time; micro strain values confirm material homogeneity and rule out significant lattice defects.
XRD UTM (HCF) Williamson-Hall MATLAB
