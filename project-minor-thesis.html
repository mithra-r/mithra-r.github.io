Parametric CAD study of drain hole shapes in a gas turbine shaft — balancing fluid drainage with structural integrity

Design Context

  • Drain holes required in gas turbine shafts to prevent oil/condensate accumulation
  • Problem: any hole creates a stress concentration that reduces fatigue life
  • Goal: find hole shape that minimises stress concentration factor (SCF) while maintaining drain function
  • Material: Inconel 718 — Ni-based superalloy for high-temperature applications

Hole Geometries Studied

  • Circular — baseline (SCF = 3 for uniaxial tension, per Kirsch)
  • Elliptical — varying semi-axis ratio a/b
  • Rectangular with corner radii — AR = width/height, varying corner radius
  • Racetrack — semi-circular ends with straight mid-section
AR 2–2.5
Rectangular hole with optimised corner radius — minimum SCF found

●FEA & Stress
Stress Analysis using Abaqus CAE & HyperMesh
Finite element analysis of stress concentration around drain holes under torsion and bending loads

FEA Setup

  • Meshing: HyperMesh — structured hex mesh, refined around hole
  • Solver: Abaqus CAE — Standard (implicit)
  • Element type: C3D8R (8-node reduced integration hexahedra)
  • Loads: combined torsion + bending, representing shaft operating conditions
  • Boundary conditions: fixed end + load application point

Results Extracted

  • Peak von Mises stress at hole edge
  • Stress Concentration Factor: SCF = σ_max / σ_nominal
  • SCF calculated per hole geometry and AR
  • Contour plots for each configuration

Key Insight

There is an optimal aspect ratio window for rectangular drain holes. Below AR=2, the hole is too compact (high curvature → high SCF). Above AR=2.5, the long sides create stress risers. The AR 2–2.5 range balances both effects optimally.

AR 2–2.5
Rectangular hole with rounded corners → minimum SCF in gas turbine shaft

Design Recommendation

  • Use rectangular drain holes with corner radius (not circular)
  • Target AR between 2.0 and 2.5
  • Orient long axis perpendicular to principal stress direction
  • Corner radius ≥ 15% of hole width to avoid corner stress peaks
  • Applicable to Inconel 718 shafts in high-temperature turbine environments
