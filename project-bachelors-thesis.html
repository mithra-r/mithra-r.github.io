X-ray Diffraction & Lattice Characterisation
Characterising microstructure of 18% Ni maraging steel using XRD and Williamson-Hall analysis

18% Ni Maraging Steel — Background

  • Ultra-high strength steel: yield strength up to 2400 MPa
  • Used in aerospace: rocket casings, high-stress structural members
  • Strengthening mechanism: precipitation of Ni₃Mo, Ni₃Ti intermetallics during ageing
  • Key challenge: optimising heat treatment for fatigue performance

XRD Analysis — Williamson-Hall Method

  • Measures crystallite size (D) via peak broadening
  • Measures microstrain (ε) from lattice distortion
  • W-H plot: β·cos(θ) vs 4·sin(θ) — slope gives strain, intercept gives size
  • Scherrer equation used as cross-check for D
  • Phase identification: martensite / austenite ratio

High Cycle Fatigue Testing
S-N curve generation and failure analysis of 18% Ni steel specimens under rotating bending

Test Configuration

  • Test type: rotating bending fatigue (R = −1, fully reversed)
  • Specimens: machined from heat-treated stock, ground and polished
  • Stress levels: multiple amplitudes to generate S-N curve
  • Run-out criterion: 10⁷ cycles (HCF regime)
  • Multiple specimens per condition for statistical validity

Heat Treatment Conditions Tested

  • Solution annealed only (baseline)
  • Aged at varying temperatures (460°C, 480°C, 510°C)
  • Aged for varying durations (3h, 6h, 9h)
  • Over-aged condition — precipitation coarsening

●Structural Analysis
Lattice Parameters vs Fatigue Life Correlation
Establishing the relationship between XRD-derived microstructural parameters and HCF performance

Correlation Methodology

  • XRD parameters (D, ε, dislocation density) extracted per specimen batch
  • Fatigue life (N at constant stress) measured per same batch
  • Statistical correlation: Pearson R between lattice params and log(N)
  • Trend analysis across heat treatment conditions

Key Structural Findings

  • Smaller crystallite size → higher fatigue strength (Hall-Petch effect)
  • Higher microstrain (from precipitation) → improved HCF life up to optimal ageing
  • Over-ageing: coarser precipitates → reduced fatigue resistance
  • Peak fatigue performance at intermediate ageing condition
